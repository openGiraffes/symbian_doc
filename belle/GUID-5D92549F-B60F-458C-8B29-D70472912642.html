Extra keys

Product- or application-specific keys

Used for other product-specific purposes.

The recommended or optional keys shown in Table 4 can be added to a product to emphasise or facilitate some functions. They can be used to control applications or hardware such as spoken commands, sound recording, and audio volume control.

Table: Key definitions

Keys

Description

Camera

Turns Camera on and takes pictures.

Power

Turns the device on and off, and can be used. For example, to access the profiles list (for quickly switching the active profile).

Voice

For voice dialing.

Volume
If a device has dedicated volume keys, they replace the scrolling keys’ functionality in adjusting volume. A short press on a volume key adjusts the volume by one level; a long press performs a continuous operation. Adjusting volume with the dedicated volume keys affects:

  • applications that are in the foreground (and use volume);

  • applications (such as a player) that are active in the background;

  • active applications in the background, regardless of whether the foreground application uses volume or not.

The keys do not affect applications that are inactive in the background.

Zoom

For zooming content. For example, in the camera viewfinder or in the image viewer.

Dedicated keys for controlling media (audio and video).
